De Cádiz a Hanalei

From Cádiz to Hanalei

An interactive lyric map

De Cádiz a Hanalei traces one rhythm, el compás, across five centuries and three oceans: from Spanish bolero through Caribbean son to Hawaiian reggae. Click any pin to read the verse it inspired and the historical chain that connects 1717 Cádiz to tonight in Hanalei.

Luke Sonson is Hawaiian and Jacobo Alvarez Martinez is Spanish, so we built the song around the musical lineage they share. Louis Murray (UK) and Robby Meek (Singapore) come from cultures that did not fit cleanly into this particular combination, but we were all drawn to the broader question of how music shifts as it travels across cultures.

De Cádiz a Hanalei

Verse 1 Cádiz, Spain · the embarkation Jump to map →
En el puerto de Cádiz amanece el galeón,
con la guitarra al hombro y el sol en el bolsillo.
Yo no llevo equipaje, solo llevo un compás,
y el compás es un mar que no se acaba jamás.
In the port of Cádiz the galleon dawns,
With my guitar on my shoulder and the sun in my pocket.
I carry no luggage, only a rhythm,
And the rhythm is a sea that never ends.
Verse 2 La Habana, Cuba · the son cubano is born Jump to map →
Llegué a La Habana, le di la cuerda al son,
el bongó respondió como un nuevo corazón.
La clave dijo "tres-dos, tres-dos" en la pared,
y desde aquella noche, el mundo bailó al revés.
I arrived in Habana, I tuned up the son,
The bongo answered like a new heart.
The clave said "three-two, three-two" against the wall,
And from that night, the world danced backwards.
Verse 3 Monterey, Alta California · the vaqueros depart, 1832 Jump to map →
De Monterey salieron tres jinetes del cantar,
con la silla mexicana y el lazo en el costal.
"Adiós, mi Alta California, voy a una isla del mar
donde un rey, Kamehameha, me ha llamado a enseñar."
From Monterey three riders of song set out,
With the Mexican saddle and the lasso in their packs.
"Goodbye, my Alta California, I go to an island in the sea
Where a king, Kamehameha, has called me to teach."
Bridge Lahaina, Maui · the arrival, 1832 · the music shifts Jump to map →
Y cuando llegué a Lahaina, en mil ochocientos treinta y dos,
dejé caer la guitarra y se levantó otra voz.
El viento Kauaʻula me cantó al desembarcar,
y el compás aprendió a caminar de otra manera…
And when I arrived in Lahaina, in eighteen thirty-two,
I let the guitar fall and another voice rose up.
The Kauaʻula wind sang to me as I stepped ashore,
And the rhythm learned to walk in another way…

Pre-Chorus the language and meter shift
From Cádiz to Habana, from California to Lahaina,
the ship kept moving — not north, but west, then west again,
till the Spanish guitar got loosened and the bolero became one drop,
and a wave broke at Mākaha and the rhythm wouldn't stop.

De Cádiz a Hanalei — na ka makani, na ka mele —

from one-two-three to one-and-two — aloha keeps it right.

Verse 4 Waimea & Mauna Kea · the slack-key opens Jump to map →
On the slopes of Mauna Kea, where the paniolo ride,
the Spanish guitar got loosened and the slack-key opened wide.
Kī hōʻalu in the morning, in the Kīpuʻupuʻu wind,
and the cattle walked to Kawaihae like the rhythm wouldn't end.
Verse 5 Honolulu / Waikīkī Shell · May 6, 1979 — Bob Marley arrives Jump to map →
Nineteen seventy-nine, the Lion came to town,
Waikīkī under the moon, the Wailers laying it down.
A one-drop in the islands — well, the islands knew the sound —
and a kid in the back row started writing his own.
Verse 6 Mākaha, Oʻahu · Israel Kamakawiwoʻole Jump to map →
On the leeward side at Mākaha, where the brother once sat down,
he picked up an ʻukulele and he turned the world around.
"Over the rainbow," soft as the trade-wind in the palm,
and the reggae of the islands carried Iz on every wave.
Verse 7 Hanalei, Kauaʻi · the music settles in, today Jump to map →
And tonight in Hanalei, where the moon is on the bay,
a kid with a slack-key ʻukulele plays a one-drop all the way.
He doesn't know the journey, but the journey knows him well —
from Cádiz to Hanalei, every wave's a story to tell.

aloha mai, aloha aku, mai Kepānia a hiki i Hanalei.
Here is the voice, here is the fire,
Love comes, love goes, from Spain (Kepānia) all the way to Hanalei.
